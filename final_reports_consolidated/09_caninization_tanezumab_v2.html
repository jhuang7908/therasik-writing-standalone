Executive Summary
Caninization of Tanezumab-V2 to dog IGHV3-9*01 / IGKV3-18*02 scaffolds is assessed as PASS. All six CDR loops are preserved at 100% sequence identity. Structural modeling (Boltz-2) confirms the caninized Fv retains near-identical backbone geometry relative to the human parental antibody (global Fv Cα RMSD 0.447 Å; CDR RMSDs all ≤ 0.36 Å; VH-VL packing angle Δ 0.5°). A systematic interface analysis demonstrates that no caninization framework substitution disrupts any dog NGF contact residue. The minor pI decrease (8.29 → 6.18) reflects canine germline FR composition and is expected to normalize toward the clinical range upon conjugation with Dog IgGB Fc. Two sequenced-level advisory flags (pI and instability index) are noted for downstream expression validation but do not constitute design failures.

Scaffold Selection Rationale
IGHV3-9*01 was selected as the VH scaffold based on CDR length compatibility and highest FR1–FR3 alignment among Tier 1 canine germlines. The lower overall FR identity (~68%) relative to human (IGHV3-23-class) is expected for cross-species caninization and is within the accepted range for the graft_vernier strategy. Six Vernier positions (VH FR2/FR3) were back-mutated to the human donor to maintain CDR structural support. IGKV3-18*02 was selected for VL; no Vernier back-mutations were required. FR4 regions were replaced with the Universal Pet FR4 (UP-FR4) clinical set derived from approved veterinary mAbs.
