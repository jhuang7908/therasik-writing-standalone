Dysregulated innate immune responses drive inflammatory diseases, yet clinical translation of mouse innate immunity research remains limited by species differences in immune signaling. Studying human inflammatory reactions in vivo is challenging due to inadequate human myeloid cell engraftment in existing humanized mouse models. Here we show that NSG-QUAD mice expressing four human myelopoiesis transgenes (IL-3, GM-CSF, SCF, CSF1) reconstituted with human CD34+ hematopoietic stem and progenitor cells develop functional human innate immune cells capable of mounting NF-κB, type I interferon, and NLRP3 inflammasome responses to lipopolysaccharide challenge. Human monocytes, dendritic cells, and granulocytes engrafted in peripheral blood, spleen, and liver by 6 weeks post-injection. Humanized NSG-QUAD mice responded to intraperitoneal and intranasal LPS with production of NF-κB-dependent cytokines including TNF-α, IL-6, IL-12p70, and IL-8, as well as type I interferon. LPS challenge induced NLRP3 inflammasome-mediated IL-1β and IL-18 secretion in serum and tissues. Pharmacological validation demonstrated that MCC950 specifically abrogated IL-1β and IL-18 production without affecting NF-κB-dependent cytokines or human monocyte viability, confirming that inflammasome activation occurs independently of pyroptotic cell death. These data demonstrate that humanized NSG-QUAD mice provide a competent in vivo platform for studying NF-κB, type I interferon, and inflammasome effectors of human innate immunity, enabling preclinical evaluation of human-specific innate immune therapeutics.
