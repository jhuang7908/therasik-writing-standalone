Background: Dysregulated innate immune responses drive the pathogenesis of numerous inflammatory diseases, yet clinical translation of preclinical findings remains challenging due to fundamental differences between mouse and human immunity. NSG-QUAD mice express four human myelopoiesis transgenes (IL-3, GM-CSF, SCF, and CSF1) to enhance engraftment of human innate immune cells, but whether these mice support functional human inflammatory responses in vivo remains unknown.

Methods: We reconstituted NSG-QUAD mice with human CD34+ hematopoietic stem and progenitor cells and assessed human myeloid cell development in peripheral blood, spleen, and liver by flow cytometry at 6 weeks post-transplantation. We challenged humanized mice with lipopolysaccharide via intraperitoneal (15 μg) and intranasal (6 mg/kg) routes and measured NF-κB-dependent cytokines, type I interferon, and inflammasome-derived cytokines in serum, bronchoalveolar lavage fluid, and tissue supernatants. We evaluated NLRP3 inflammasome specificity using the small-molecule inhibitor MCC950 (50 mg/kg).

Results: Humanized NSG-QUAD mice displayed robust engraftment of human monocytes, dendritic cells, and granulocytes across peripheral blood, spleen, and liver. LPS challenge induced human NF-κB-dependent cytokines (TNF-α, IL-6, IL-12p70, IL-8) and type I interferon (IFN-α) in both systemic and mucosal compartments. LPS also triggered NLRP3 inflammasome activation, evidenced by IL-1β and IL-18 secretion. MCC950 specifically abrogated IL-1β and IL-18 production without affecting NF-κB cytokines or monocyte viability, demonstrating that inflammasome activation occurs independently of pyroptotic cell death.

Conclusion: Humanized NSG-QUAD mice support functional human NF-κB signaling, type I interferon responses, and NLRP3 inflammasome activation in vivo, providing a translational platform for studying human innate immunity and testing inflammasome-targeted therapeutics.
