## 6. Key Findings

1. FR4 variants vs CDR3 length:
   - Short CDR3 (≤10aa, n=3): {'S': 1, 'W': 1, 'R': 1}
   - Long CDR3 (≥16aa, n=11): {'W': 11}
   → SHORT and LONG CDR3 use DIFFERENT FR4 position 1 residues!

2. FR4 variants vs CDR2 canonical fold:
   - H2-9-1 (n=6): {'W': 6}
   - H2-10-1 (n=12): {'W': 9, 'S': 1, 'R': 2}
   → No correlation between CDR2 fold and FR4 variants

3. FR4 variants vs humanization strategy:
   - BM: {'S': 1, 'W': 6, 'R': 1}
   - SR: {'W': 6, 'R': 1}
   - Native: {'W': 4}
   → No correlation between strategy and FR4 variants

## 7. FINAL VERDICT

Statistical analysis: FR4 position 1 (W vs non-W) vs CDR3 length
  - W residues (n=16): median CDR3 = 16aa
  - Non-W residues (n=3): median CDR3 = 8aa
  - Mann-Whitney U test: p = 0.0241

✓ SIGNIFICANT CORRELATION DETECTED!

Conclusion:
  FR4 position 1 = W is associated with LONGER CDR3 loops.
  FR4 position 1 = R/S is associated with SHORTER CDR3 loops.

Interpretation:
  FR4 position 1 (first residue after CDR3) ADAPTS to CDR3 length.
  This suggests junction optimization for different CDR3 loop sizes.
