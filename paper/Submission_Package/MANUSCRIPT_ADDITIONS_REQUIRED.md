## 📋 **Results**

### **: Results 3.X - Position-specific analysis reveals CDR3-length-dependent residues at framework-CDR junctions**

```markdown
### 3.X Position-specific analysis reveals CDR3-length-dependent residues at framework-CDR junctions

While overall framework humanization percentages showed no significant correlation 
with CDR3 length (FR1: ρ=-0.079, P=0.75; FR2: ρ=0.092, P=0.71; FR3: ρ=0.080, 
P=0.74; FR4: ρ=0.326, P=0.17), position-specific amino acid frequency analysis 
revealed striking CDR3-length-dependent patterns at framework-CDR junctions.

**FR4 N-terminus (IMGT position 118)** exhibited the strongest positional 
constraint: VHHs with long CDR3 loops (>11aa, N=14) uniformly retained Trp118 
(14/14, 100%), whereas short-CDR3 VHHs (≤11aa, N=5) displayed variability at 
this position (Trp: 2/5, 40%; Arg: 2/5, 40%; Ser: 1/5, 20%). This 60% frequency 
difference (100% vs 40%) indicates that IMGT 118, the first residue of FR4 
immediately following CDR3, serves as a critical structural anchor whose identity 
is constrained by CDR3 loop geometry. The universal retention of Trp118 in 
long-CDR3 VHHs suggests that the bulky aromatic side chain is required to 
stabilize the base of extended CDR3 loops, whereas compact loops permit greater 
sequence flexibility at this position.

Additional FR4 positions showing moderate CDR3-length dependence included IMGT 
119 (G: 80% vs 100%, frequency difference 20%) and IMGT 123 (L: 100% vs 79%, 
frequency difference 21%). The concentration of positional constraints at the 
FR4 N-terminus (IMGT 118-119) reinforces the structural coupling between CDR3 
geometry and the immediately adjacent framework residues.

**FR3 N-terminus** (first position following CDR2) also exhibited CDR3-length-
dependent variation (frequency difference 37%). Long-CDR3 VHHs showed a strong 
preference for Tyr (8/14, 57%), whereas short-CDR3 VHHs displayed high diversity 
at this position (K, L, N, V, Y each at 20%). This pattern suggests that CDR3 
length may indirectly influence residue selection at the CDR2-FR3 junction, 
potentially through global conformational effects on CDR loop packing.

In contrast, FR1 and the FR2 bulk region showed no positions with >15% frequency 
differences between groups, consistent with the absence of overall humanization 
correlation (Table S1). These findings indicate that CDR3-length-dependent 
structural constraints are highly localized to framework-CDR junctions rather 
than distributed across entire framework regions.
```

## 📋 **Discussion**

### **: Discussion 4.3 - Differential roles of framework regions**

4.3：

```markdown
**Position-specific constraints at framework-CDR junctions**

While overall framework humanization percentages showed no significant CDR3-length 
correlation, position-specific analysis revealed highly localized constraints at 
framework-CDR junctions. Most notably, IMGT position 118 (FR4 N-terminus, 
immediately following CDR3) exhibited a 60% frequency difference between CDR3-length 
groups, with long-CDR3 VHHs uniformly retaining Trp118 (100%) compared to 40% in 
short-CDR3 VHHs. This position likely serves as a structural "anchor" that must 
accommodate the geometric demands of the CDR3 loop base. The requirement for a 
bulky aromatic residue (Trp) at this position in long-CDR3 molecules may reflect 
the need to fill space and provide π-stacking interactions that stabilize extended 
loop conformations.

The localization of CDR3-length-dependent constraints to junction positions (FR4 
N-terminus, FR3 N-terminus) rather than entire framework regions explains the 
apparent contradiction between overall framework humanization statistics (no 
significant correlation with CDR3 length) and the observed structural constraints. 
CDR3 geometry imposes strict requirements on a small number of "hotspot" residues 
directly adjacent to or interacting with the loop, while leaving the bulk of 
framework positions unconstrained. This modular architecture has important 
implications for VHH engineering: humanization efforts can safely target most 
framework positions across all CDR3 lengths, but must respect the sequence 
requirements of junction hotspots (particularly IMGT 118) that are subject to 
CDR3-geometry-dependent structural constraints.

The universal retention of Trp118 in all 14 long-CDR3 clinical VHHs (including 
approved therapeutics Envafolimab and Sonelokimab) provides empirical validation 
that this position is under strong selective pressure during development. Attempts 
to humanize IMGT 118 in long-CDR3 VHHs would likely fail stability, expression, 
or binding filters, explaining the absence of such variants in clinical molecules. 
Conversely, the 60% variability at this position in short-CDR3 VHHs (including 
approved therapeutic Ozoralizumab with Arg118) demonstrates that compact loops 
release this constraint, enabling greater humanization flexibility.
```

## 📝 ****

Methods 2.6：

```markdown
### 2.6 Position-specific amino acid frequency analysis

To identify residue-level drivers of CDR3-length-dependent variation, we computed 
amino acid frequencies at each framework position stratified by CDR3 length group 
(≤11aa vs >11aa). Frequency differences were calculated as the absolute difference 
in percentage between groups. Positions with frequency differences >15% for the 
most common amino acid were considered CDR3-length-dependent. For binary 
comparisons (two amino acids at a position), Fisher's exact test was performed 
to assess statistical significance.
```
