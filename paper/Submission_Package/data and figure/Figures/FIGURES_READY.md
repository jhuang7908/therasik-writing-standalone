## Figure Legends (for Manuscript)

### Figure 1 Legend:
**Figure 1. Phylogenetic Analysis of VHH Framework Regions.**

**(A)** FR2 region phylogenetic tree (N=19 VHHs + 4 references). VHHs are labeled with CDR3 length (L=X) and CDR2 fold (H2-9-1 or H2-10-1). Colors indicate CDR3 length groups: Red (≤11 aa), Green (>11 aa). Reference sequences: Human IGHV3-23\*01 and IGHV3-13\*01 (humanization targets), Alpaca IGHV3-3\*01 (VHH origin), Mouse IGHV1-72\*01 (outgroup). Tree construction: UPGMA clustering on normalized Hamming distance. Note the clear segregation of short-CDR3 VHHs toward the human reference cluster (P = 0.00175, Kruskal-Wallis test).

**(B)** FR2+FR3 combined phylogenetic tree showing the global humanization landscape. The tree demonstrates a gradient from fully humanized (Class 1, short CDR3, 93.2% human identity) to partially camelid-retained (Class 2, long CDR3, 87.9% human identity). Mouse IGHV1-72 serves as the outgroup root. Spearman correlation between CDR3 length and phylogenetic distance from Human IGHV3-23: ρ = -0.604, P = 0.0062 (FR2 alone); ρ = -0.486, P = 0.035 (FR2+FR3 combined).

---

### Figure 2 Legend:
**Figure 2. Sequence Logo Analysis of Framework Regions Stratified by CDR3 Length.**

Sequence conservation plots for FR1, FR2, FR3 (first 20 aa), and FR4, comparing Short CDR3 (≤11 aa, N=5) vs. Long CDR3 (>11 aa, N=14) groups. Letter height represents amino acid frequency at each IMGT-numbered position. 

**(A-B)** FR1 shows high conservation across both groups.

**(C-D)** FR2 shows group-dependent differences at key positions. In particular, IMGT position 50 is **Arg (R)** in 13/14 long-CDR3 VHHs (93%), whereas the short group more frequently substitutes to **Leu (L)** or **Pro (P)** (Table S1). Additional differences are observed at IMGT positions 49, 52, 54, and 55.

**(E-F)** FR3 shows high conservation across groups. In our dataset, IMGT positions 93–95 and 104 are conserved across both groups (Table S1).

**(G-H)** FR4 shows standard J-region sequence with minor junction variations due to CDR3 length differences. No systematic correlation with CDR3 length or humanization strategy.

**Key Interpretation**: FR2 contains the primary CDR3-length-dependent variation in this dataset, with a strong group difference at IMGT position 50. FR3 positions are comparatively conserved.
