## Manuscript Summary

VHHs (nanobodies) have emerged as a transformative therapeutic modality, with four molecules now approved for clinical use and over 50 candidates in development pipelines. However, the structural determinants governing successful humanization—a critical step to minimize immunogenicity—remain poorly understood. Traditional classifications into "CDR-grafting" versus "Resurfacing" strategies lack mechanistic insight and fail to explain the diversity of clinical VHH sequences.

In this study, we performed the **first comprehensive retrospective analysis** of 19 clinical-stage VHHs (4 approved, 15 in clinical trials) to identify the true constraints on humanization. Using phylogenetic clustering anchored with human, alpaca, and mouse germline references, we discovered that:

1.  **FR2 humanization is strictly gated by CDR3 length**, with an 11-amino-acid threshold distinguishing molecules that achieve full humanization (93.2% human identity) from those structurally constrained to retain camelid features (87-88%).
2.  **Position 47 (IMGT) acts as a molecular "solubility switch"**, with Arg47 conserved in 93% of long-CDR3 VHHs but substituted to Leu/Pro in short-CDR3 variants.
3.  **FR1 and FR3 are universally humanized** (>87% human identity) regardless of CDR3 length or design strategy, revealing them as "pre-humanized" scaffolds shared between camelids and humans.

These findings **overturn the strategy-based paradigm** and establish a structure-driven classification system grounded in phylogenetic evidence.

## Significance and Novelty

This work addresses a critical gap in antibody engineering by:
- **Providing actionable design rules**: Engineers can now predict whether aggressive FR2 humanization is viable based solely on CDR3 length, de-risking development timelines.
- **Establishing a three-class system**: Replacing ambiguous labels ("Native," "Resurfacing") with data-driven classifications based on natural sequence clustering.
- **Revealing the mechanistic basis of FR2 constraints**: The Arg47 "solubility valve" explains why long-CDR3 VHHs resist humanization, linking sequence evolution to structural necessity.

Our findings are immediately relevant to the **50+ VHHs currently in clinical development** and will inform rational design of next-generation nanobodies with optimal developability and reduced immunogenicity risk.
